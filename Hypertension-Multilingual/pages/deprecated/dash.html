What is the DASH Eating Plan?

DASH stands for Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension. It's not a temporary diet, but a flexible and heart-healthy eating style that's proven to help lower high blood pressure.

‚¨ÜÔ∏è Eat More Of:

Foods rich in Potassium, Calcium, Magnesium, Fiber, and Protein.

  • Fruits & Vegetables
  • Fat-Free or Low-Fat Dairy
  • Whole Grains, Fish, Poultry, Beans, Seeds & Nuts

‚¨áÔ∏è Limit:

Foods that can raise blood pressure or harm your heart.

  • Sodium (Salt)
  • Sugar-Sweetened Drinks & Sweets
  • Foods High in Saturated Fat (like fatty meats & full-fat dairy)

Become a Sodium Detective

Reducing sodium is a cornerstone of the DASH plan. Most sodium comes from processed and restaurant foods, not the salt shaker.

üè∑Ô∏è

Read the Label

Choose foods with a low % Daily Value (DV) for sodium. 5% or less is low, while 20% or more is high.

üõí

Choose Fresh

Select fresh or frozen vegetables without added sauces. Pick fresh poultry and fish over cured or processed meats.

üåø

Flavor with Spices

Use salt-free spice blends, herbs, lemon, lime, vinegar, or wine to add flavor to your food instead of salt.
