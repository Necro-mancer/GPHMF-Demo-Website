What is Heart Failure?

A diagnosis of heart failure can feel overwhelming, but understanding what it means is the first step. It does not mean your heart has stopped‚Äîit means your heart muscle has become weaker and needs support to pump enough blood for your body's needs.

A Healthy, Strong Heart

A healthy heart is a powerful, efficient pump that supplies your entire body with the oxygen and nutrients it needs to thrive.

  • Strong Muscle: Pumps blood with adequate force.
  • Normal Size: Not enlarged or thickened.
  • Efficient Pumping: Ejects over half the blood from its main chamber with each beat (Ejection Fraction >50%).

A Weakened Heart

In heart failure, the heart muscle is weakened or stiff and cannot pump effectively. This causes a "backup" of fluid and reduces blood flow to the body.

  • Weak/Stiff Muscle: Struggles to pump or fill properly.
  • Often Enlarged: The heart may grow bigger to try to compensate.
  • Reduced Pumping: Ejects less blood with each beat (Ejection Fraction often <40%), leading to symptoms.

Your Roadmap for Care: The Stages

Your doctor uses stages to create the best treatment plan for you. Think of it as a roadmap. The goal is to help you live well and stay in the earliest stage possible for as long as possible.

A

Stage A: At Risk

You are at high risk due to conditions like high blood pressure or diabetes, but have no symptoms or heart problems yet.

Your Goal: Prevent heart disease with healthy lifestyle choices.

B

Stage B: Pre-Heart Failure

You have a structural heart problem (like a weak pump) but have never had symptoms of heart failure.

Your Goal: Protect your heart with medications and lifestyle to prevent symptoms from ever starting.

C

Stage C: Symptomatic

You have been diagnosed with heart failure and have (or previously had) signs and symptoms.

Your Goal: Manage symptoms, improve your quality of life, and prevent hospital stays.

D

Stage D: Advanced

You have advanced symptoms that don't get better with treatment.

Your Goal: Work with a specialized team to maintain comfort and quality of life.
