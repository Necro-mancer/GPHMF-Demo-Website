You Have Options: Finding Your Path to Quit

Everyone's journey is different. Combining different methods often leads to the best results. Explore the options below to see what might work for you.

Nicotine Replacement Therapy (NRT)

NRT gives you nicotine in controlled doses without the other harmful chemicals in tobacco. This helps manage withdrawal symptoms. It comes in patches, gum, lozenges, inhalers, and nasal sprays. Most are available over-the-counter.

Prescription Medications

Drugs like Varenicline (Chantix) and Bupropion (Zyban) are non-nicotine pills that help reduce cravings and withdrawal symptoms. Talk to your doctor to see if these are right for you.

Counseling & Coaching

Working with a trained counselor can help you develop coping strategies, identify triggers, and build a solid quit plan. This can be done one-on-one or in a group setting.

Quitlines & Apps

Free telephone quitlines (like 1-800-QUIT-NOW in the US) provide expert coaching. Smartphone apps can offer tracking, motivation, and a community of support right at your fingertips.

Friends & Family

Let your loved ones know about your decision to quit. Their encouragement and understanding can be one of your most powerful tools.
