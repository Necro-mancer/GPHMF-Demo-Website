Take Control of Your Health

Managing your weight is one of the most powerful steps you can take to manage diabetes and live a healthier, more energetic life.

Why Weight Management is Key for Diabetes

When you have diabetes, carrying extra weight, especially around your waist, can make it harder for your body to use insulin effectively. This is called insulin resistance. Losing even a modest amount of weight can have a huge impact, helping your body respond better to insulin, making it easier to manage your blood sugar levels, and reducing your risk of complications.

The Diabetes Plate Method

A simple, visual way to build healthy meals without counting or measuring. All you need is a 9-inch plate.

1. Half with Non-Starchy Vegetables

These are low in carbs and high in nutrients and fiber. Examples: spinach, carrots, broccoli, tomatoes, and salad greens.

2. A Quarter with Lean Protein

Protein helps keep you full and satisfied. Examples: chicken or turkey without the skin, fish like salmon, eggs, beans, and tofu.

3. A Quarter with Carbohydrate Foods

These foods impact blood sugar most. Focus on whole grains and starchy vegetables. Examples: brown rice, quinoa, whole-wheat pasta, potatoes, and corn.

4. Add Water or a Zero-Calorie Drink

Staying hydrated is key. Water is the best choice. Unsweetened tea or coffee also work well.

Mindset for Success: Real Stories, Real Motivation

The journey is different for everyone. Find inspiration in these stories.

Carlos's Story: A New Family Recipe

"Food is love in my family. I was afraid I'd have to give up our traditions."

Instead of giving up his favorite meals, Carlos learned to adapt them. He started using brown rice in his family's rice and beans, baking chicken instead of frying it, and adding a large salad to every dinner. He discovered he could still enjoy the flavors he loved while taking control of his blood sugar.

Sarah's Story: From Fear to Fitness

"I hadn't exercised in years. I didn't know where to start and was worried about my blood sugar dropping."

Sarah's doctor suggested she start with a 10-minute walk after her biggest meal of the day. She checked her blood sugar before and after to feel secure. Soon, 10 minutes became 20, and she found she had more energy than ever. The walks became her favorite way to de-stress and manage her health.
